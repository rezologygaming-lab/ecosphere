Environmental Topics

🌊

Ocean Acidification

Since the Industrial Revolution, ocean pH has dropped by 0.1 units — a 26% increase in acidity. This disrupts marine food chains, coral bleaching, and shellfish development. The ocean absorbs ~30% of atmospheric CO₂.

Greenhouse Effect

Natural vs enhanced greenhouse effect. CO₂, CH₄, N₂O and water vapor trap heat, driving global warming beyond natural cycles.

Wildfires & Ecosystems

Climate change intensifies wildfire frequency and scale. Forests store carbon — burning them releases centuries of stored carbon in days.

EcologyCarbon Cycle
🌱

Soil Health

Healthy soil holds 3x more carbon than the atmosphere. Intensive farming, erosion, and chemical use threaten this critical ecosystem.

AgricultureCarbon Sink
🧊

Cryosphere & Feedback Loops

Ice reflects sunlight (albedo effect). As Arctic ice melts, dark ocean water absorbs more heat, accelerating warming further — a dangerous positive feedback loop. Permafrost thaw releases methane, a greenhouse gas 80x more potent than CO₂ over 20 years.

HISTORY & PROJECTION

Climate Through Time

1750

Industrial Revolution Begins

Coal-powered industry starts injecting CO₂ into the atmosphere. Pre-industrial CO₂: 280 ppm.

1896

Arrhenius Predicts Global Warming

Swedish scientist Svante Arrhenius first calculates that doubling CO₂ would raise global temperature by 5–6°C.

1958

Keeling Curve Launched

Charles Keeling begins measuring atmospheric CO₂ at Mauna Loa, creating the most important dataset in climate science.

1988

IPCC Founded

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change established. NASA scientist James Hansen testifies to Congress about global warming.

2015

Paris Agreement

196 countries commit to limiting warming to 1.5–2°C above pre-industrial levels. A landmark in global environmental policy.

2050

Net Zero Target

The global goal: achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2050. Requires transforming energy, transport, agriculture, and industry.
